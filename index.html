Stages of Sleep

Sleep can be categorized into new major categories: Rapid Eye Movement (REM) Sleep and Non-REM Sleep. Non-REM sleep is further classified into 4 stages. Stages 3 and 4 together are known as Slow Wave Sleep (SWS).

REM

Rapid Eye Movement

Your eyes move.

REM - RAPID EYE MOVEMENT

During this stage of sleep, your muscles become completely paralyzed and the majority of your memorable and vivid dreams occur. REM is a vital component in memory consolidation, and if deprived of REM sleep, there will be a noticeable REM rebound.

Non-REM Stage 1

This is a very light stage of sleep that is thetransition from wakefulness to sleep. It is very short, and easily disrupted.

Sleep Spindles and K- Complexes!

Non-REM Stage 2

The most time of sleep is spent in stage 2, due to passing through the stage multiple times moving up into REM sleep and down into SWS. This stage is characterized by sleep spindles and k-complexes, which seem to correlate with memory consolidation and information processing in the brain.

Slow Wave Sleep

This stage corresponds to deep sleep, and it is often hard to wake a person from this stage. It is characterized by large amplitude, low frequency waves of brain activity, hence the name of slow wave sleep. As you get older, you tend to get less slow wave sleep.

Gain Weight

Increase in unhealthy weight and high chances of diabetes

Sleep deprivation leads to imbalances in hormones, including those of hunger. The hormone, leptin, which usually inhibits appetite is not as apparent when sleep deprived, and ghrelin, the hormone that increases appetite becomes more apparent. Even worse, this causes the body to crave unhealthy high-carb foods.
